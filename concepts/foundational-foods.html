A purpose-driven model of an ideal human diet, ordered by biological intent rather than industrial priorities or scientifically reductive recommendations. Foods are organized into tiers based on their role in sustaining life and vitality.

Tier 1 Foods

Direct provisioning for offspring. Meant specifically to nurture a new life from zero to complete.

  • Mammalian Milk
  • Eggs
  • Honey/Royal Jelly (fast energy)
  • Seeds/Nuts (slow energy)

Tier 2 Foods

Nutrient-dense whole animals to sustain the organism through stages of life after provisioning (the finished product of Tier 1).

  • Muscle Meat
  • Animal Fat
  • Organs
  • Marrow/Blood
  • Shellfish

Tier 3 Foods

Sugar-rich and fat-rich foods that are made specifically to attract animals for dispersal/pollination.

  • Fruit
  • Nectar
  • Sap

Tier 4 Foods

Supporting structural/functional plant parts that are used as defenses, photosynthetic machinery, or storage organs.

  • Tubers
  • Roots
  • Stems
  • Leaves
  • Bark

Tier 4 foods are consumed for variety/diversity and used temporarily or instrumentally in the following ways:

  • Supplements: to replenish what is missing.
  • Adaptogens: to balance what is stressed.
  • Therapeutics: to heal what is broken.
  • Nootropics: to enhance and provide clarity to the mind.
  • Psychotropics: to transform and integrate toward awakening.
