--- title: Truth tags: concept description: Truth is what beauty (and therefore coherence) looks like from the outside. ---

Truth is what beauty (and therefore coherence) looks like from the outside. It is the cold, masculine expression of beauty, as opposed to goodness, which is its warm, feminine expression.

It is what appears in (and is accessible to when translated into) thought, structure, and logic. Truth lacks the relational embededness (inside felt coherence) of being attuned to the whole. It sits outside of coherence and reflects it, from the perspective of external observation, describing its parts and relations through distance, abstraction, and representation.

Truth is relative, unless it is guided by goodness. When relative truth is guided by absolute goodness, it becomes capital-T Truth, that is, truth made whole through its integration into a greater coherence. The total coherence of Truth serving goodness radiates beauty, and Truth becomes not just accurate but in "right relationship" and alive.

In isolation (without serving goodness), facts and logic can be weaponized. It is what causes story to become narrative rather than vision. Powerful, but can justify its manipulation and eventually seeks to dominate rather than harmonize.
