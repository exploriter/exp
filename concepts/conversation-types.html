--- title: Four Types of Conversations tags: ["concept", "collection"] description: The difference between dialogue, discourse, debate, and diatribe. ---

Healthy Types

Structure Style Goal
Dialogue 2-way, cooperative (win-win). Mutual listening with openness of mind and spirit. Understanding and relationship-building.
Debate 2-way, competitive (win-lose). Argumentative engagement focused on finding flaws. Persuasion and rhetorical victory.
Discourse 1-way, cooperative. Structured presentation aimed at clarity and coherence. To inform, explain, or educate.
Diatribe 1-way, competitive. Emotionally charged expression, often attacking or rallying. To provoke, intimidate, or inspire.

Failure Modes

Structure Style Goal
Manipulation 1-way, reason-steering, covertly competitive. Performatively polite, strategic, selectively responsive. Steer judgment while preserving the appearance of choice.
Sophistry 1-way, reason-dominating, overtly competitive. Clever, adversarial, performative. Win, dominate, or discredit rather than discover truth.
Propaganda 1-way, sense-shaping, covertly competitive. Explanatory, authoritative, framing-heavy. Fix interpretation by pre-structuring meaning, narrative control.
Instigation 1-way, sense-driving, overtly competitive. Incendiary, moralized, emotionally charged. Mobilize affect toward coordinated action.

Original four-quadrant chart and descriptions are adapted from David W. Angel.
